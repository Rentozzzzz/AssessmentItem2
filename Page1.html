What is the Event Horizon?

The ‘event horizon’ is the boundary defining the region of space around a black hole from which nothing (not even light) can escape. In other words, the escape velocity for an object within the event horizon exceeds the speed of light. The name arises since it is impossible to observe any event taking place inside it – it is a horizon beyond which we cannot see. The event horizon therefore effectively hides the singularity at the centre of the black hole, a boon for astrophysicts discomforted by the breakdown of the laws of physics at such a point.

For a non-rotating black hole, the event horizon is a spherical surface. For a rotating black hole, the event horizon is an oblate spheroid, or a shape like a squashed sphere. The event horizon is the point of no return for anything that falls into a black hole. Once something crosses the event horizon, it is lost to the black hole forever.

What is the Singularity?

Some black holes trap more and more material as their mass increases. The point where all that mass is trapped is called a singularity. It may be infinitely small, but its influence is enormous. Imagine a circle with a singularity in the middle.

As the circle gets smaller and smaller, the singularity gets bigger and bigger. The singularity is the point where the laws of physics as we know them break down. What is the Schwarzschild Radius?

The Schwarzschild radius is the radius of the event horizon surrounding a non-rotating black hole. It is named after the German astronomer Karl Schwarzschild, who calculated this exact solution for the theory of general relativity in 1916.
