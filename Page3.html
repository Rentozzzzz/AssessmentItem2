What are some Black Hole Theories that are currently circulating within the Scientific Field

Well, there's quite a lot of them, but we will only cover a few, the following theories we will be briefly covering are

  • The Information Paradox
  • Thermodynamics of the Black Hole
  • Black Hole Entropy
  • Black Hole Complementarity

What is The Information Paradox?

The black hole information paradox is a conflict between two apparently incontrovertible facts: first, that semiclassical gravity is valid on scales where gravitational and quantum effects are more or less separate; second, that quantum mechanics is “unitary” and thus all quantum processes are in principle, though not necessarily in practice, reversible.

Thermodynamics???

Yes, the basic idea of black hole thermodynamics is that the irreversibility that occurs when an object is absorbed by a black hole is similar to the statistical irreversibility that is familiar in ordinary physics. When a body falls into a black hole, entropy increases.

You just talked about Entropy, what is it?

Well, Black hole entropy, also known as Bekenstein-Hawking entropy, is a measure of the disorder or uncertainty associated with a black hole, proportional to the area of its event horizon, and crucial for understanding black hole thermodynamics. In general, entropy is a measure of the disorder or randomness of a system. The more disordered a system is, the higher its entropy.

Lastly, what is the Black Hole Complementarity

Black hole complementarity is a theoretical framework suggesting that information about matter falling into a black hole is preserved and can be described from both the outside and the inside without contradiction. It posits that observers outside the black hole can use a unitary framework, while those inside experience a different reality, making both perspectives valid in their respective contexts. This concept aims to resolve the black hole information paradox by indicating that no information is lost, as it can be retrieved from the black hole's exterior through Hawking radiation.
